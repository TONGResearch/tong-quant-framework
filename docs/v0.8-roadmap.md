# Tong Quant Framework V0.8 Roadmap

V0.8 implements the Notification Engine only. The remaining boundaries below
continue to reserve future modules without implementation.

Post-V0.8 engineering stabilization hardens notification leases, dead letters,
credential persistence controls, database migrations, transactions, and PIT
documentation without opening a new functional milestone.

Paper Trading, Execution Engine, Fund Layer, and Asset Allocation remain
outside V0.8.

V0.7.1 hardening must remain in force before V0.8 begins:

- Execution mode defaults to `disabled`.
- ExecutionDisabledGuard and ExecutionReadinessGate fail closed.
- Risk consumes generic risk-position inputs.
- Portfolio/Risk artifacts can be reconstructed from SQLite by proposal id.
- Notification compatibility is research-artifact oriented only.

## Notification Engine

Implemented scope:

- Convert approved research artifacts into human-readable alerts.
- Support Telegram, WeChat, and Email integrations.
- Preserve manual review workflows before any future execution step.
- Consume ResearchReport, ValidationReport, PortfolioProposal, and
  RiskAssessment artifacts.
- Persist generated messages to a SQLite outbox before any delivery attempt.
- Support disabled, preview, and enabled modes, defaulting to disabled.
- Deduplicate by artifact hash, channel, and recipient.
- Load provider credentials only from environment variables.
- Record delivery attempts without storing credentials or unsafe provider
  errors.
- Include the mandatory research-information disclaimer in every message.

Boundary:

- Must not create orders.
- Must not connect to brokers.
- Must not convert alerts into automatic actions.
- Must not consume Order, Trade, Broker, Fill, or Execution objects.
- Channel adapters consume rendered NotificationMessage objects only.

## Paper Trading

Future scope:

- Simulate portfolio proposals and future execution decisions without capital.
- Track hypothetical fills, slippage assumptions, and research-to-action quality.
- Separate paper results from validated historical research.

Boundary:

- Must not use live brokerage connectivity.
- Must not be treated as proof of execution readiness.
- Must remain downstream of Research, Validation, Portfolio, and Risk.

## Execution Engine

Future scope:

- Convert approved, validated, risk-checked decisions into execution-side
  instructions.
- Support future QMT, PTrade, IBKR, and Moomoo adapters.
- Preserve manual approval where required.

Boundary:

- Must be the only layer allowed to create execution-side objects.
- Must not be bypassed by Research, Validation, Portfolio, Risk, AI, or
  Notification modules.
- Must remain disabled until explicitly approved in a later milestone.

## Fund Layer

Future scope:

- Reserve support for ETF, Mutual Fund, REIT, Bond Fund, and Money Market Fund
  instruments.
- Extend discovery, screening, research, validation, portfolio, and risk models
  beyond equity-only assumptions.
- Preserve the existing equity-focused V1.0 workflow.

Boundary:

- Must not implement fund research in V0.7.
- Must not introduce fund allocation logic in V0.7.
- Must not change current equity workflows into multi-asset workflows yet.

## Asset Allocation

Future scope:

- Support future allocation research across equities, funds, cash, and reserved
  alternative assets.
- Use market regime, risk budget, concentration, liquidity, and scenario stress
  as decision variables.
- Keep allocation outputs explainable and validation-aware.

Boundary:

- Must not create automatic rebalancing.
- Must not bypass Portfolio and Risk review.
- Must not become an execution instruction.
